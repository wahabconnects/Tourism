The exact population of Metropolitan Lagos is disputed.[35] In the 2006 federal census data, the conurbation had a population of about 8 million people.[36] However, the figure was disputed by the Lagos State Government, which later released its own population data, putting the population of Lagos Metropolitan Area at approximately 16 million.[note 1] Daily, the Lagos area is growing by some 3,000 people, or around 1.1 million annually, so the true population figure of the greater Lagos area in 2022 is roughly 28 million (up from some 23.5 million in 2018). Lagos may therefore have overtaken Kinshasa as Africa's most populous city. As of 2015, unofficial figures put the population of "Greater Metropolitan Lagos", which includes Lagos and its surrounding metro area, extending as far as into Ogun State, at approximately 21 million.[1][27][37][38]

We have listed some of the fun places to hang out in Lagos; The Bogobiri House The Bogobiri House introduces visitors to the artistic side of Lagos. This boutique hotel is a gathering place for those who enjoy music and art. It’s a welcoming spot for both leisure and business travellers who want to unwind and catch up with friends. Bogobiri conjures many historical memories, making it an exciting destination to stay. Escape the hustle and bustle of Lagos and feel at home while surrounded by art and delicious food.
Bogobiri House If you haven’t had the chance to sample some of the best dishes in the country, Bogobiri House offers you an opportunity to forget civilization and lick your fingers after your meals. In the night, the place comes alive with music. Karaoke, and live bands.
The Old Lagos The ancient landmarks of the Old Lagos called Ìsàlẹ̀ Èkó still maintain their significances in the city. You can visit Old Lagos, where the modern-day Lagos Marina is situated. Take a walk around the central business centre of Lagos, where the Brazilian-Cuban architecture buildings and shipping port have their homes. Soak up the beautiful edifices, a sunset view of the city, companies with their head offices, and the oldest Angelical cathedral in the country.
Lagos Beaches Lagos boasts several beaches that have grown popular with visitors. If you want to experience a bit of water-world paradise in the country, you can explore the pristine beaches in Lagos. Each beach provides an unforgettable experience, with a wide range of activities to indulge in. You can relax with a glass of wine, go shell picking, enjoy a picnic, participate in various water sports, and plan your own activities.
fun places to hang out
Some of the popular beaches to visit include Landmark beach, Ilashe Beach, Tarkwa Bay, la Campagne beach resort, Oniru beach, and Lekki Leisure Lake. These beaches are a must-see due to their luxury and exclusivity. You may forego driving and instead take a boat to several of these beach resorts.
Eko Atlantic Eko Atlantic is a city in Lagos, well-planned on reclaimed land from the Atlantic Ocean. It offers some of the most spectacular architectural designs you can ever see in Nigeria. The future of Lagos is Eko Atlantic with its smart modern real estate and advanced infrastructures. You and your friends can visit and explore this futuristic city during weekends.
Lekki Conservation Centre & Lufasi Park If you believe Lagos is all about hustle and bustle and the never-ending movement of automobiles, you’re in for a surprise. Lagos enables you to observe nature through its eyes. The city has numerous green parks, especially the Lekki Conservation Centre and Lufasi Park.
fun places to hang out in lagos
Lufasi Park is a 20-hectare urban green zone with pristine forests. It boasts a scenic natural spot to rejuvenate your spirit and a beautiful lake. Visitors who’re looking for thrilling adventures can visit the Lekki Conservation Centre, which has Africa’s longest canopy walk. Make sure you add these two parks to your bucket list. You would always want to come to them whenever you visit Lagos.
